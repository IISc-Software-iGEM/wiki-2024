Collab with Steam Vision Foundation and Ladakh Science Foundation

Our iGEM team collaborated with two science foundations to promote synthetic biology and computational biology across schools and colleges. Our goal was to ensure that students from diverse backgrounds had the opportunity to engage with these fields, sparking interest and providing educational experiences regardless of their prior exposure. The participants were students from Ladakh, Srinagar and Jammu, Madhya Pradesh, Assam, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Bengaluru, Meerut and more.

To reach different age groups effectively, we tailored our programs to suit their learning needs. For high school students (classes 9 through 12), we organized two sessions called "Beyond the Microscopes," offered both online and offline. These sessions were designed to move beyond conventional textbook teaching, introducing students to real-world applications of synthetic biology and computational biology. We emphasized hands-on learning, helping students visualize DNA and protein structures through tools like PyMOL, computational tools to translate DNA fragments.

For younger students in grades 5 through 8, we developed an interactive program called "BioMania." This initiative made learning fun, simplifying complex concepts in biology and encouraging students to think like young scientists. Through engaging craft activities, we aimed to cultivate curiosity and inspire these students to explore science further in the future.

For college students, we took a different approach by hosting a Tech-Bio Ideathon." This event encouraged participants to think critically and come up with innovative solutions to problems in synthetic biology. It was an opportunity for students to apply what they had learned in a creative, collaborative environment, connecting biology with real-world challenges. The judges for this ideathon were people from across industries and academia including professors, entrepreneurs, PhD students, doctors etc. We had maximum participation by women showcasing their growing interest in STEM. By fostering a space for innovative thinking, we aimed to empower college students to explore synthetic biology in a way that could lead to tangible contributions in the field.

Through these outreach efforts—spanning younger students to college-level thinkers— we aimed to make synthetic biology accessible, exciting, and inclusive. By conducting sessions across age groups and using both online and offline formats, we ensured that students from different backgrounds, geographical areas, and educational levels could participate and feel inspired to be part of the future of science.

Sowing seeds for future iGEMers

Our team is not only focused on promoting Synthetic and Computational Biology but also committed to fostering future iGEMers at IISc Bengaluru. By involving first-year undergraduates in a collaborative project, we provided hands-on experience that blended Engineering and Science to create meaningful software solutions.

The project introduced key topics like protein structures and functionalities, alongside practical tools like PyMOL for visualization. We also emphasized core software engineering skills such as Python packaging, Git, and command-line tools. This inclusive approach led to the development of Bept, a user-friendly tool for protein electrostatics aimed at benefiting the scientific and the iGEM community.
