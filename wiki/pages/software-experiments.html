All vs Subset of Crosslinks

We run our pipeline by choosing random subsets of crosslinks, since we are solving a localization problem which is basically the supporting framework for the two bodies, its solution is not unique (depends on random seed), hence final solution is also not unique and directly depends on structure of supporting framework.

We also tried by choosing all crosslinks, but that resulted in more clashes between proteins and percentage of satisfied crosslinks was less. This is because our registration algorithm will force every crosslink to be equal to the distance specified but that is not true in for all crosslinks. Hence we choose random subsets of crosslinks to get better results.

Evaluation

We get different solutions to localization problem for different runs, which results in different registered structures. We evaluated these structures based on percentage of correctly satisfied crosslinks.

A crosslink is said to be satisfied if crosslink distance is less than 35 angstroms. In our case crosslink distance is between CA atoms between specific residues of 2 chains.
